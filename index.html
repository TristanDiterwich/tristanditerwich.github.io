Note about recycling

Recycling ICE vehicles

Recycling ICE vehicles is common practise. Companies like ARN state that they can recycle up to 98% of a car. Valuables like batteries, fluids, tyres, plastics and more are taken from the car and sent to partners for specialised recycling. After all valuables are removed, the car moves through the shredder. The different pieces of material are separated by their raw material. Then the Post-Shredder-Technology melts the scraps together to form usable raw materials.

Recyclling Electric vehicles

Electric cars are recycled in the same way, expect for the hard-to-recycle battery. However, recycling electric car batteries is rapidly developing. Start-ups like EcarAccu promise to recycle up to 98% of electric car batteries. They check every cell for damage, leakage and capacity. The cells that pass the test are sold to sellers who need a lot of cheap batteries. This can for example be the owner of a solar power plant who wants to store electricity for the night. The rest of the battery is disassembled into raw materials.
